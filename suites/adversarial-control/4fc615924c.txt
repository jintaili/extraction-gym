Our suggested starting recipe for this coffee:
18g coffee in, 300g water out, 2:45–3:00 total brew time on a flat-bottom brewer.
Grind a little finer than you would for a natural Ethiopian if you want more lemon curd sweetness.

This washed lot comes from Danche, a small washing station near Chelbesa in the Gedeo zone of Yirgacheffe. Local smallholder farmers deliver ripe cherry grown on garden plots at high elevation, where cool nights help preserve the florals we look for in coffees from this area.

After hand sorting, the coffee is depulped, fermented in clean water, washed through channels and dried slowly on raised beds. The cup is delicate and precise: jasmine in the aroma, white peach sweetness, a bright lemon-curd acidity and a clean black-tea finish.
