This lot comes to us from Marta Elena Ramirez's Finca La Esperanza outside San Marcos in the department of Ocotepeque, western Honduras. Please note: this is the Honduran San Marcos area, not San Marcos, Guatemala; the farm sits near the Salvadoran border on cool, pine-covered slopes.

On the cupping table we find a bright mandarin acidity up front, a soft brown-sugar sweetness through the middle, and a red-apple clarity as the cup cools. The finish is tidy and cocoa-like, making this an easy morning filter coffee with enough structure for longer extractions.

Marta picks the Pacas and Catuai cherries selectively, depulps them the same afternoon, ferments the parchment for 18–24 hours, and washes in clean spring water before slow drying on raised beds. We roast this one light to preserve the citrus and apple character while keeping the body rounded.
