This release from El Diviso is all about saturated fruit and a polished, perfumed finish. The cup opens with raspberry jam and pink grapefruit before settling into cacao nib and a gentle rose-like florality as it cools.

Nestor Lasso and the team at El Diviso have become known for careful, repeatable fermentation work in Huila. For this Ombligon selection, ripe cherries were hand sorted, held in a sealed fermentation environment, and then dried slowly as a natural lot until the desired moisture and water activity were reached.

We roast this coffee for filter brewing: enough development for sweetness and structure, while keeping the vivid acidity and floral aromatics intact. We recommend brewing 15g coffee to 250g water, starting around 94°C.
