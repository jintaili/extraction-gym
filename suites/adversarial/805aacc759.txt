Tasting Notes: red currant, orange marmalade, black tea, cane sugar

Origin: Rwanda
Region: Nyamasheke, Western Province
Producer: Kanzu Washing Station + surrounding smallholder farmers
Process: Washed
Variety: Bourbon
Altitude: 1,800–2,100 masl
Harvest: June–August 2024
Roast: Light

This lot comes from the Kanzu washing station on the steep green hills above Lake Kivu. Farmers deliver hand-picked cherry in the afternoon, and the station team sorts, depulps, ferments, washes, and slow-dries the parchment on raised beds.

In the cup we find the classic sparkle we love from western Rwanda: red currant up front, orange marmalade sweetness through the middle, and a clean black-tea finish. We roast this coffee for clarity and sweetness as a filter-forward profile, though it also makes a lively long black.

Recommended brew: 16g coffee / 260g water, 94°C, medium-fine grind.
