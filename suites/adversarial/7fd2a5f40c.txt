This coffee comes to us from Finca Las Nubes, a family-run farm tucked into the high ridges above San Rafael Las Flores in the department of Santa Rosa, Guatemala. Santa Rosa can be a confusing name on a coffee map — there are well-known coffee towns with the same name in other producing countries — but this lot is from southeastern Guatemala, where volcanic soils and cool nights help the cup stay sweet and structured.

Marta Elena Pérez and her team pick ripe cherries in several passes, depulp the coffee the same day, and ferment it in tiled tanks for 18–24 hours before washing and drying on raised beds. The result is a balanced washed Guatemala with a clean citrus lift and a soft brown-sugar sweetness.

We roast this lot for filter brewing. Expect a medium-light profile that opens up nicely on V60, Kalita, and batch brew.

Recommended brewing: 1:16 ratio, 93°C water, paper filter.
