Variety: SL28, SL34, Ruiru 11, Batian
Altitude: 1,600–1,900 masl
Harvest: January–February 2025

Gichathaini sits on the southern slopes of Mount Kenya, where smallholder members deliver ripe cherry to the factory on the same day it is picked. This AB lot was dry fermented, washed in clean river water and slowly dried on raised tables until stable.

In the cup we find classic Nyeri intensity: vivid blackcurrant up front, a bright pink-grapefruit snap through the middle, and a clear cane-sugar finish. We roast this one for filter brewing, with enough development to keep the acidity structured and sweet.

Recommended for: V60, Kalita Wave, Aeropress and batch brew.
