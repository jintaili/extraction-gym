This lot comes from Finca El Zapote in Santa Rosa, Guatemala. Santa Rosa is one of those place names you will see on coffee bags from more than one origin, so for clarity: this is the department in south-eastern Guatemala, on the slopes around Pueblo Nuevo Viñas, not a Honduran Santa Rosa lot. The farm’s high elevations and cool nights give a clean, structured cup with a soft floral opening and a brown-sugar finish.

The García family pick Bourbon and Caturra cherries by hand and deliver them to the farm’s small wet mill the same afternoon. After depulping, the coffee is fermented in tiled tanks, washed with spring water and dried slowly on raised beds until stable for export.

In the cup we find orange blossom aromatics, a crisp red apple acidity and panela sweetness. We like it on V60, Kalita or any paper-filter brewer where the washed clarity can shine.
