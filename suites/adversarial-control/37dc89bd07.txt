Origin: Kenya
Region: Nyeri County, Mathira
Producer: Gichathaini Factory, Gikanda Farmers Cooperative Society
Process: Washed
Variety: SL28, SL34, Ruiru 11, Batian
Altitude: 1,600–1,900 masl
Roast: Light / Filter
Harvest: December 2024–January 2025

Tasting Notes: blackcurrant, grapefruit marmalade, demerara sugar, rosehip

Gichathaini Factory sits near the town of Karatina in Nyeri and is one of the washing stations operated by the Gikanda Farmers Cooperative Society. Smallholder members deliver ripe cherry during the main harvest, and the factory team sorts, depulps, ferments, washes, and slow-dries the parchment on raised tables.

We bought this AA separation for its classic Nyeri structure: bright citrus acidity, deep blackcurrant sweetness, and a long, tea-like finish. It is roasted light for filter brewing, where we find the florals and syrupy red-fruit character are most expressive.

Brew suggestion
Dose: 16g coffee / 260g water
Water: 203°F, soft filtered
Grind: medium-fine for pour-over
