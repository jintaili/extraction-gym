This lot comes from Finca Los Andes in the Santa Bárbara department of Honduras, a high green ridge above Lake Yojoa. Santa Bárbara is a name you will also see on maps in other coffee-producing countries, but this coffee is firmly Honduran: grown near Peña Blanca and delivered through a small mill network focused on clean washed lots.

Miriam Agurcia’s family keeps the picking tight, separating ripe cherries through the peak of the harvest. After depulping, the coffee is fermented in tiled tanks, washed with spring water, and dried slowly on raised beds under shade netting.

In the cup we find red apple and panela sweetness up front, with a lifted orange-blossom aroma and a tea-like finish. We roast this as a bright but balanced filter profile.
