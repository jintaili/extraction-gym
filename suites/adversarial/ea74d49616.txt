Bright, tropical and very limited.

Tasting notes: lulo sorbet, pink lemonade, vanilla wafer

Producer: Finca La Pradera — Oscar Daza
Region: Aratoca, Santander, Colombia
Process: Anaerobic Natural
Variety: Castillo, Colombia
Altitude: 1,700 masl
Roast: Filter
Harvest: March–May 2024

This small lot from Finca La Pradera is one of the most expressive coffees we have bought from Santander this season. Expect a cup that opens with sharp citrus and tropical fruit, then settles into creamy vanilla sweetness as it cools.

For this release, ripe whole cherries were placed into sealed stainless tanks for a slow anaerobic fermentation. During that tank stage, the farm team added fresh lulo juice and a little panela syrup so the fruit aromatics could mingle with the coffee before it was moved to raised beds for careful drying. The result is playful and unmistakably fruit-forward, while still carrying the structure we love from Castillo and Colombia grown in this part of Aratoca.

We roast this coffee lightly for filter brewing. Try it as a V60 or Kalita with a slightly cooler brew temperature if you want to keep the lemonade-like acidity crisp.

About the producer
Finca La Pradera is managed by Oscar Daza and his family in Santander, Colombia. Their program focuses on meticulous cherry selection, controlled fermentations and slow drying to produce highly distinctive microlots.
