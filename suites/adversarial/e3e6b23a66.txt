This is the new season selection from Hacienda La Pradera in the mountains above Aratoca, Santander. The Daza family have spent years refining their picking and drying protocols, and this Pink Bourbon separation is one of the most aromatic lots we cupped from Colombia this season.

Cherries were hand sorted, floated, and then dried as a natural process lot on raised beds under shade. The slow drying keeps the cup clean while building a jammy red-fruit sweetness. Expect strawberry jam up front, a round cacao nib sweetness through the middle, and a pink grapefruit lift as the coffee cools.

We roast this coffee for filter brewing. It works beautifully as a V60, Kalita or batch brew; if you prefer espresso, we recommend starting with a longer ratio and allowing extra rest after roast.

About the farm
Hacienda La Pradera is operated by the Daza family near Aratoca in Santander, Colombia. The farm focuses on separated varieties and careful post-harvest handling, with shaded drying beds and meticulous moisture control for small lots like this Pink Bourbon.
