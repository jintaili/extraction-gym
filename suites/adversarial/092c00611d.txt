A tiny lot from María Fernanda López in the hills above Palestina, Huila. This Gesha was hand-picked at peak ripeness, fermented briefly in cherry, depulped, washed in clean spring water, and dried slowly on raised beds under shade cloth.

In the cup we find a clean floral sweetness: jasmine aromatics, white peach, bergamot and a long honeyed finish. It is delicate but not thin, with a silky texture that shines on pour-over and other manual brew methods.
